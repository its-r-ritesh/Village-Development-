Village Infrastructure Development



To reduce drudgery of women and to bring prosperity into villages through road connectivity are the focus area of SVADES. Hence, major funds of SVADES are directed towards development of infrastructure in the villages

like Water Supply Schemes, Construction of Roads, Repairs and Renovation of Aanganwadies and Primary Schools and Low Cost Housing facilities. SVADES has laid 10.50 KMs of critical link roads interconnecting 13 villages. These roads are a boon to the villages, especially in monsoon.

Rural villages surrounding to the industrial belt, have none or inadequate sources of safe drinking water. SVADES has initiated as well as commissioned village level water supply schemes. Apart from constructing underground water tanks and bore wells, SVADES has laid some 11.16 KMs of pipelines in 8 villages benefiting over 2200 families.

Under the Sardar Awas Yojana, 128 houses have been constructed in 10 Panchayat villages for families below the poverty line.




Education and Skill Development



India has many initiatives to develop education and skills, including the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) and Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS). PMKVY is the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship's flagship program, which aims to train young people in skills across the country. As of September 2021, PMKVY has trained more than 13 million people in various sectors. JSS, which is implemented by NGOs, provides vocational training to non-literates, neo-literates, and school dropouts in rural areas.

The COVID-19 pandemic has led to a rapid shift to digital-learning models, which has increased the need for digital assets, infrastructure, upskilling of teachers, and content. The Indian education and skill development sector has also seen an accelerated growth.

90% of jobs in India are skill-based, though only 6% of the workforce is trained. Skill development can help students develop holistic skills, such as adaptability, entrepreneurship, problem-solving abilities, self-confidence, and life skills. This can help students prepare to thrive in a competitive world where versatility and adaptability are highly prized.




Healthcare Services



India's rural health care system is made up of three tiers, including Primary Health Centers (PHCs), Community Health Centers (CHCs), and mobile dispensaries. PHCs are the first point of contact for the village community and the medical officer, and they provide preventive and curative health care to the rural population. CHCs are referral centers for four PHCs and provide facilities for specialist consultations and obstetric care. CHCs have 30 in-door beds, one OT, X-ray, labor room, and laboratory facilities. Mobile dispensaries also provide people with access to good health treatment.

The government also provides public health facilities in villages, including: Panchayat hospitals, Free medicines, and Cataract camps.

PHCs are established and maintained by the State Governments under the Minimum Needs Programme (MNP)/ Basic Minimum Services Programme (BMS). PHCs are trained in dealing with common illnesses under the supervision of a doctor. Village health workers provide counseling and evaluate psychological problems, referring patients with serious mental illness to health professionals.




Agricultural Development



Agricultural development is the process of creating conditions that allow farmers to reach their agricultural potential. This includes providing support, such as: Security, Research, Advanced techniques, Pest control, and Diversity.

Other ways to boost agricultural development include: Increasing agricultural yield per unit of land, Alleviating poverty, and Aligning agriculture with food security needs.

Community Development



Rural community development is a branch of community development that focuses on improving the lives of people living in rural areas. It focuses on social issues, particularly community organizing.

Community development is a holistic approach that aims to empower community members and create stronger and more connected communities. It involves the principles of:

sustainable development, empowerment, inclusivity, social justice, human rights, participative democracy, and equality.

The Village Community Development Society (V.C.D.S.) is an organization that was established in 1980 and has been a member of Emmaus International since 1991.
